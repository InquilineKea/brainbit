# Collagen Variants Analysis Summary

## Overview

This analysis examined collagen gene variants in your genome, with a special focus on COL1 (COL1A1, COL1A2) and COL7 (COL7A1) genes. Collagen is the most abundant protein in the human body and is a major component of connective tissues.

### Key Findings

- **Total collagen variants found**: 23,703
- **High-impact collagen variants**: 75
- **Primary collagen gene variants (COL1A1, COL1A2, COL7A1)**: 131

### COL1A2 (Type I Collagen Alpha 2 Chain)

  - Genotype: Homozygous (1/1)

## Clinical Relevance

### COL1A2 p.Pro549Ala Variant
- This missense variant changes a proline to alanine in the collagen triple helix
- Proline residues are critical for the proper folding of collagen proteins
- This variant is homozygous (present on both chromosomes)
- While classified as "moderate impact," this variant:
  - Is located in a region important for collagen stability
  - May potentially affect collagen fiber formation and strength
  - Could influence connective tissue properties

### Other High-Impact Collagen Variants

You have several other high-impact variants in collagen genes that may be relevant:

1. **COL14A1** - One HIGH impact splice acceptor variant
   - Important for regulating collagen fibril assembly
   - Associated with connective tissue strength

2. **COL5A3** - Seven MODERATE impact missense variants
   - Type V collagen is found in tissues containing type I collagen
   - Important for regulating fiber diameter in connective tissues

3. **COL6A5** - Nine variants including one HIGH impact frameshift
   - Associated with muscle and skin disorders
   - The frameshift variant (p.Leu2591fs) may significantly alter protein function

## Potential Health Implications

### Type I Collagen (COL1A1/COL1A2)
Type I collagen is the most abundant collagen in the human body, found in:
- Skin (80% of skin collagen)
- Bones
- Tendons
- Ligaments
- Cornea
- Blood vessel walls

Variants in these genes are associated with:
- Osteogenesis Imperfecta (brittle bone disease)
- Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome (classical type)
- Caffey Disease

### Type VII Collagen (COL7A1)
Type VII collagen is the main component of anchoring fibrils that attach the epidermis to the dermis. Variants in this gene are associated with:
- Dystrophic Epidermolysis Bullosa
- Epidermolysis Bullosa Acquisita

## Recommendations

Based on the identified variants:

1. **For COL1A2 p.Pro549Ala variant**:
   - Monitor bone health and density
   - Consider connective tissue evaluation if symptoms arise
   - Be aware of potential increased risk for connective tissue disorders

2. **For other collagen variants**:
   - Be aware of potential skin elasticity and wound healing implications
   - Monitor for joint hypermobility or instability
   - Consider these variants when evaluating any connective tissue symptoms
